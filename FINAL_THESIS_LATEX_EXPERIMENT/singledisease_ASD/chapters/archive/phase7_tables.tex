\toprule
\thead{Justification Dimension} & \thead{Specification} & \thead{Reference / Rationale} \\
\midrule
\endfirsthead
\endhead
\bottomrule
\endlastfoot
Total sample size               & 131 unique subjects + 20{,}000 images across 6 biomedical domains & Exceeds all minimum thresholds for planned analyses (regression, SEM, DL, bootstrap) \\
\addlinespace
Per-domain minimum              & Smallest domain: PD ($n = 50$); largest: ASD ($n = 300$) & PD sample augmented via SMOTE and data augmentation; bootstrap valid for $n \geq 30$ \citep{efron1993bootstrap} \\
\addlinespace
Power analysis rationale        & No formal a priori power calculation (secondary data research) & Justified: datasets are fixed-size public repositories; researcher cannot control $n$; post-hoc power $> 0.95$ for all domains with observed effect sizes \\
\addlinespace
Why bootstrap (not parametric)  & Bootstrap resampling with 1{,}000 iterations and 95\% CI & Distribution-free: EEG/gait features violate normality assumptions (Shapiro--Wilk $p < .001$ for ASD); bootstrap makes no distributional assumptions \\
\addlinespace
Why 1{,}000 resamples           & \citet{efron1993bootstrap}: 1{,}000 sufficient for 95\% CI; 10{,}000 for 99\% CI & Stability verified: ASD ensemble varied $< 0.3\%$ between 1{,}000 and 10{,}000 resamples across 5 re-runs; diminishing returns beyond 1{,}000 \\
\addlinespace
Why not Monte Carlo / MCMC      & Study uses non-parametric bootstrap, not Monte Carlo simulation or Markov Chain Monte Carlo & Bootstrap resamples observed data; Monte Carlo generates synthetic data from assumed distributions; MCMC requires Bayesian priors --- neither appropriate for DL classification validation \\
\addlinespace
Cross-validation complement     & Stratified $k$-fold ($k = 10$) + Leave-One-Subject-Out (LOSO) & $k$-fold provides variance estimate; LOSO provides subject-independent generalisation; bootstrap provides CI without distributional assumptions; three methods triangulate \\
\addlinespace
PD small-sample mitigation      & Augmentation via SMOTE (1:1 class balance), bootstrap CI width reported, sensitivity analysis with $n = 30$ and $n = 50$ subsamples & Limitation explicitly acknowledged: PD accuracy (100\%) may be inflated by small $n$; CI width (95.2--100\%) is narrower than expected, warranting caution \\
\addlinespace
Reproducibility                 & Fixed seed (42), version-pinned libraries, ICC $= 0.97$ across 5 independent re-runs & All bootstrap samples deterministic with seed; results reproducible on any hardware with identical preprocessing pipeline \\
\addlinespace
\midrule
\multicolumn{3}{@{}p{\dimexpr\textwidth-2\tabcolsep}}{\textit{Summary: 131 unique subjects (768+ data instances) plus 20{,}000 cancer images exceed all statistical thresholds. Bootstrap selected over parametric methods due to non-normal feature distributions. 1{,}000 resamples validated as sufficient for 95\% CI stability. PD small-sample limitation acknowledged with sensitivity analysis. Monte Carlo/MCMC explicitly excluded.}} \\
\end{xltabular}
