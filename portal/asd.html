B2C — 12 Stages: Description & Data (Card View)
1Problems — Description
P1: ASD diagnosis is delayed by an average of 4.5 years, causing children to miss the critical early intervention window (ages 2–5) where therapy is most effective.
P2: Clinical EEG costs $5,000+ per session; consumer wearable headsets cost $300–$800 but lack validated AI frameworks.
P3: Parents receive binary "ASD/Normal" results with no explanation of how the AI reached its conclusion.
P4: 70% of rural communities in developing countries have zero access to pediatric neurologists.
1Problems — Data
P1: Mean diagnosis age = 4.5 years (CDC, 2023); optimal intervention = 18–36 months
P2: Clinical EEG = $5,000; Emotiv Insight = $299; EPOC X = $799
P3: Consumer explainability = 0% (no current tool provides governed explanations)
P4: Neurologist density = 0.03 per 100K in rural India vs 4.8 per 100K in urban US
2Gaps — Description
G1: No unified governance framework exists for consumer-grade EEG-based AI diagnosis across the full pipeline.
G2: No multi-agent orchestration governance for agentic AI in healthcare.
G3: No explainability standard exists for wearable-based diagnosis (SHAP + RAG + consumer-friendly language).
G4: Consumer wearable EEG signals not validated against clinical-grade EEG.
G5: No unified quality taxonomy combining ML, GenAI, and governance.
G6: No benchmark exists for measuring diagnostic effort reduction.
2Gaps — Data
G1: Existing frameworks reviewed: 15 (NIST, ISO, EU AI Act, etc.) — none cover full pipeline
G2: Multi-agent governance papers: 0 in systematic review (2020–2025)
G3: RAG faithfulness in healthcare: untracked in current tools
G4: Consumer vs clinical EEG correlation studies: 3 (none with ASD focus)
G5: Max taxonomy size in literature: ~50 modules vs RGAIG+ 525 modules
G6: Effort reduction benchmarks: 0 published for wearable ASD screening
