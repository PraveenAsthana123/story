What is RGAIG+?

The Problem (B2C)

Over 1 billion people worldwide suffer from neurological disorders. Current diagnostic pathways require specialist visits (6-18 month wait), expensive equipment ($50K-200K clinical EEG), and expert interpretation. Consumer wearable EEG ($300-800) can democratize diagnosis but lack governance, trust, and AI safety.

  • 970 million with mental health disorders (WHO)
  • 50 million with epilepsy worldwide
  • 10 million new Parkinson's cases annually
  • 1 in 36 children diagnosed with ASD (CDC)
  • 280 million with depression globally
The Solution

RGAIG+ is a 9-layer, 525-module unified framework governing the entire AI diagnostic pipeline: consumer wearable EEG → signal processing → diagnostic AI → GenAI explanations → agentic orchestration → consumer interface → clinical oversight → monitoring → governance.

Innovation: First framework integrating device-level governance with GenAI governance for consumer healthcare AI.

Validation: MSCA 90.8% ± 2.6% across 5 diseases, 768+ subjects, 305 GB real data.

Contributions: 525-module taxonomy, 10-pillar governance, agentic orchestration governance, RAG faithfulness controls.

EEG & the 10-20 System

International electrode placement standard for brain signal acquisition

International 10-20 Electrode Placement

The 10-20 system positions electrodes at 10% and 20% intervals across the scalp. Landmarks: nasion (nose bridge), inion (skull base), pre-auricular points (ears). Provides standardized spatial sampling of brain electrical activity.

Electrode positions: Fp1, Fp2 (frontal pole), F3, F4, F7, F8 (frontal), C3, C4 (central), T3, T4, T5, T6 (temporal), P3, P4 (parietal), O1, O2 (occipital), Fz, Cz, Pz (midline).

Delta (0.5-4 Hz)

Deep sleep, brain injury detection

Theta (4-8 Hz)

Drowsiness, memory encoding, meditation

Alpha (8-13 Hz)

Relaxed wakefulness, eyes closed

Beta (13-30 Hz)

Active thinking, concentration, anxiety

Gamma (30-100 Hz)

Cognitive processing, perception binding

EEG Biomarkers per Disease
Autism (ASD) — 19 channels

Atypical gamma power, reduced alpha coherence, frontal-posterior connectivity. Spectral power + connectivity features.

Parkinson's (PD) — 14 channels

Excessive beta in motor cortex, reduced theta/alpha ratio, slowed background. CNN-LSTM temporal dynamics.

Epilepsy — 32 channels

Spike-and-wave complexes, focal/generalized seizure patterns, interictal discharges. Real-time sliding window.

Stress — 14 channels

Elevated frontal beta, reduced alpha asymmetry, theta-beta ratio. Combined with ECG, EDA, PPG.

Depression — 32 channels

Frontal alpha asymmetry (L<R), increased theta, reduced prefrontal connectivity. Multi-modal features.

Fourier Transform in EEG

Converting time-domain EEG signals to frequency-domain for feature extraction

Fast Fourier Transform (FFT)

Converts time-domain EEG signal x(t) into frequency-domain X(f), revealing power at each frequency band. Essential for extracting spectral features used by ML/DL models.

X(f) = ∑ x(t) · e-j2πft/N,   t = 0, ..., N-1
  • Window: Hamming window (2-4s epochs, 50% overlap)
  • Resolution: Δf = fs/N (e.g., 0.5 Hz at 256 Hz, 512 samples)
  • Output: Power Spectral Density (PSD) per frequency band
  • Bands extracted: Delta, Theta, Alpha, Beta, Gamma power

ISO 42001 & NIST AI RMF

How RGAIG+ extends and operationalizes international AI standards

ISO/IEC 42001:2023

AI Management System (AIMS) standard. PDCA-based. Requires: AI policy, risk assessment, objectives, resources, competence, documented processes, internal audit, management review.

RGAIG+ alignment: Layer 9 implements AIMS requirements. 525-module taxonomy maps to ISO controls. Audit trail satisfies documentation requirements.

NIST AI RMF 1.0

4 core functions: GOVERN (policies), MAP (context), MEASURE (assess), MANAGE (mitigate). Voluntary framework for trustworthy AI development.

RGAIG+ alignment: 9 layers map to all 4 functions. Layer 5 (Agentic) extends MAP. Layer 8 (Monitoring) extends MEASURE. Risk scoring extends MANAGE.

EU AI Act 2024

Risk-based regulation. 4 tiers: Unacceptable, High-risk, Limited, Minimal. Medical AI = High-risk. Requires: conformity assessment, transparency, human oversight, data governance.

RGAIG+ alignment: Framework designed for high-risk classification. Layer 7 (Clinical Oversight) = human oversight. RAG = transparency. Layer 9 = conformity.
